How to Play

Goal: destroy every enemy settlement. You lose if all your settlements fall and you have fewer than 5 units left.

Units are all one type; their role is switchable any time from the selection panel: Deploy fights at normal speed, Supply moves 2× but can't fight, Farmer grows food while working a settlement's fields. A settlement's food income comes from a built-in base worth 2 farmers, and every working farmer adds another full farmer's share on top (sheltered or garrisoned farmers don't count) — richer land makes every share bigger. There's no farmer cap, but each farmer past 10 is a little less effective than the last: by the 24th a new farmer only grows about as much as they eat, and beyond that extra farmers are a drain. Farmers are only 1/10th as tough, and run into the settlement for shelter when raiders come near.

Settlements cost 5 units to found (build on fertile land!), auto-till the ground around them, and run one production mode: Farm (grow the population), train Supply / Deploy units, or 📦 Stockpile (train nothing and bank the food). In a training mode the settlement's food surplus is spent on the unit being trained (15 food each) rather than banked, so richer income trains faster — never slower than one unit per 25 seconds while stockpiled food lasts. Farm mode grows new farmers this way while at least 50 food is stockpiled, up to the break-even crew of 24 — after that it banks the surplus, just like Stockpile mode. Farm mode never trains soldiers; set a settlement to Supply or Deploy when you want units. Every unit — farmers included — eats from the stockpile, so unit training pauses automatically when the settlement's food income only just covers what its people eat.

Food: every unit eats. Land comes in five fertility tiers (Barren → Lush). Feed armies by keeping them inside a settlement's solid team-colored territory border, toggling Pillage (a persistent stance: the army raids the highlighted area around it — standing or on the march — until you toggle it off; each moment a few random tiles in the area are stripped, and you can watch the loot fly in from the exact tiles as they degrade. Barren tiles yield nothing, so stripped land recovers very slowly and an army must move to fresh land or starve), or running a Supply route: select a supply blob, tap "Supply route", tap the army. Routes draw color-coded: green = fed, amber = falling behind, red = starving. Roughly 1 supply unit feeds 5 fighters at a quarter of the map's distance. The selection panel shows each army's fed level and whether it's trending ▲ up or ▼ down.

Hunger and combat: above ¾ fed fights best; below ½ worse; below ¼ famished (weakest), and at zero food units die within about 20 seconds.

Touch: drag to pan, pinch to zoom, tap to select. With a blob selected, tap a destination to Move right there — a plain move only moves; your units never divert to attack on the way, though they always fight back while an enemy engages them. To attack, tap an enemy blob or settlement as the target: your army follows a targeted blob while it stays in sight, and besieges a targeted settlement until it falls. A brief ring at the destination confirms every order (white = move, red = attack).

Desktop: right-click = move (on an enemy: attack it directly), left-drag = box select, WASD/edge/middle-drag = pan, wheel = zoom, Esc = cancel. Control groups: Shift+1–9 assigns the selected blobs or settlement to that number, pressing the number selects the group again, and pressing it twice quickly also centers the camera on it.
