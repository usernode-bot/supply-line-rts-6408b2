How to Play
  • Found settlements on fertile land and grow your population.
  • Feed your armies: pillage the land they stand on, or run supply routes from your settlements.
  • Wipe out the enemy to win — a side is only defeated once it has no settlements and fewer than 5 units left, too few to found a new one.
🎯 Goal & winning
  • Defeat condition: a side is out only at no settlements and fewer than 5 units left. Losing every settlement isn't the end while 5+ of your units survive: they can march somewhere fertile and rebuild (and a cornered AI will try exactly that).
  • Ruins: a razed settlement leaves a 🏚️ ruin on the map — a permanent battle scar that doesn't block anything; found a new settlement on top to clear the rubble.
🧍 Units & roles
  • One unit type: a blob's role is switchable any time from the selection panel.
  • Deploy fights.
  • Supply moves at the same speed but can't fight in the open (garrisoned in a settlement it defends the walls at half a fighter's strength) and can run supply routes.
  • Farmer grows food while working a settlement's fields — but farmers are only 1/10th as tough as other units.
  • Farmer income: new farmers walk out of the settlement to a field plot, and each earns exactly what the plot they're standing on is worth — a Lush plot pays a full share, poorer land less, and a plot below Sparse doesn't even cover what its farmer eats. Farmers claim the lushest free plot first, two farmers on one plot don't double it, and farmers still walking (or sheltered / garrisoned) earn nothing yet.
  • Working a plot directly: ordering a pure farmer group onto a friendly settlement's farmland sends them to work that field (the first hand takes the plot you tapped, the rest spread out), while tapping the settlement building itself still tucks them into the garrison for shelter.
  • No farmer cap: unworked farmland only yields a small built-in base worth 2 farmers across the whole farm — there's just a limited number of worthwhile plots.
  • Arming takes time: switching units into Deploy isn't instant — Arming takes about 10 seconds (20 at 1× speed), and until the countdown finishes they still fight — and die — as their old role. Switching away from Deploy is instant, so a disguised caravan can't flip into an army at your gates.
  • 🌱 Farmers flee danger: if a farmer group is attacked, it and every friendly farmer group within 4 tiles drops its tools and runs to garrison in the settlement — you'll get a 🌱 notice, and "Back to work" sends them out again once it's safe.
🏠 Settlements
  • Founding: settlements cost 5 units to found (build on fertile land!). Tap 🏠 Build with 5+ units selected, then pick the site on the map: on touch your first tap places a green/red 2×2 outline you can re-tap to move, and ✓ confirms; with a mouse the outline follows the cursor and a click dispatches. The army walks to the site and the 5 founders are consumed on arrival.
  • Construction: the settlement spends about 30 seconds (at 1× speed) under construction 🔨 — its health climbs from 0 to 100, enemies can attack (and even raze) it the whole time, and it produces nothing, feeds no one and trains no units until the bar is full. Once complete it auto-tills the ground around it.
  • Production modes: a finished settlement runs one mode: Farm (grow the population), train Supply / Deploy units, or 📦 Stockpile (train nothing and bank the food).
  • Training: in a training mode the settlement's food surplus is spent on the unit being trained (15 food each) rather than banked, so richer income trains faster — never slower than one unit per 25 seconds while stockpiled food lasts.
  • Farm mode grows new farmers this way until every worthwhile plot has a farmer — after that it banks the surplus, just like Stockpile mode. Farm mode never trains soldiers; set a settlement to Supply or Deploy when you want units.
  • Everyone eats: every unit — farmers included — eats from the stockpile, so unit training pauses automatically when the settlement's food income only just covers what its people eat.
  • Group building: the 5 founders don't have to march as one blob — any selection totalling 5 units or more can found a settlement. Box-select several small groups (or pick a control group), tap 🏠 Build and place the site as usual: the group nearest the site carries the founding order and everyone else marches there as escort, pooling together on arrival. If the gathered units haven't reached 5 yet, the founding party holds the site with a ⏳ "waiting for settlers" notice until the rest merge in — giving it any new order abandons the plan.
  • Town names: every settlement bears a name — Ashford, Thornmere, Ravenholt… — shown above its keep on the map and in its panel. When a new town finishes building, word travels: you'll hear rumors of the founding of a new settlement, even an enemy one. And when a town's stockpile runs dry its people starve — deaths from hunger show as 💀 skulls instead of plain damage numbers, and you're told the inhabitants of that town are starving.
🌾 Food & supply
  • Every unit eats. Land comes in five fertility tiers (Barren → Lush). Three ways to feed an army:
  • Territory: keep the army inside a settlement's solid team-colored territory border.
  • Pillage: a persistent stance — the army raids the highlighted area around it, standing or on the march, until you toggle it off. Each moment a few random tiles in the area are stripped, and you can watch the loot fly in from the exact tiles as they degrade. Barren tiles yield nothing, so stripped land recovers very slowly and an army must move to fresh land or starve. You can never pillage inside your own settlements' territory — only wild or enemy land burns.
  • Supply route: select one or more pure-supply blobs, tap "Supply route", then tap the source settlement the caravans load from, then the destination (a friendly army or settlement). Every selected blob joins the same line, and caravans on the same line merge into one when they meet on the road.
  • Settlements supply each other too: select a settlement with 🚚 units garrisoned, tap "Supply route to another settlement", then tap the destination — carriers ferry food from A to B continuously, and the route stays pinned to the source you chose.
  • Route colors: green = fed, amber = falling behind, red = starving. Roughly 1 supply unit feeds 2–3 fighters at a quarter of the map's distance.
  • Caravan raiding: every caravan shows the food it's hauling (🌾) right on the map — visible to both players — and killing an enemy caravan feeds the attacking army half of its cargo (capped by how much the attackers can carry; the rest is destroyed), so a loaded caravan is a juicy raid target.
  • Fed level: the selection panel shows each army's fed level and whether it's trending ▲ up or ▼ down.
  • Hunger: above ¾ fed fights best; below ½ worse; below ¼ famished (weakest), and at zero food units die within about 20 seconds.
  • Warnings: a mild 🍞 "Low supplies" toast when an army drops below ¾ fed (losing its combat bonus), and the 💀 "starving" alert only once it's been stuck at zero food for a couple of seconds and is actually losing units.
⚔️ Combat, sieges & healing
  • Plain moves only move: your units never divert to attack on the way, though they always fight back while an enemy engages them. A targeted army is followed while it stays in sight, and a targeted settlement is besieged until it falls.
  • Melee range: groups have no physical bulk — the circle is just for selecting, and armies walk straight through each other (crossing an enemy still starts a fight). Combat only begins at true melee range: attackers close until the crowds mingle, or right up against a settlement's walls.
  • Flanking: fighting groups show an attack-direction line: a group locked on its own target keeps facing it, and anyone hitting it from behind that line deals 50% extra damage (the link glows orange). A defender with no target of its own wheels to face its biggest attacker and splits the formation to cover more directions, so flankers striking from a clearly different side still deal a partial bonus — split your army and attack from two sides.
  • Walls: a garrisoned settlement is hard to storm — units inside its walls take only a third of open-field damage and shoot back 1.5× harder than in the open, so an even-strength assault against an armed garrison fails outright.
  • Everyone defends, weighted by role: Deploy units fight at full strength, while Supply units and farmers each fight at half — so even a town full of sheltering civilians costs the attacker real losses, but Arming the garrison still doubles its defense.
  • Sieges: while an enemy war party stands at the walls the settlement is besieged (⏳ marker): its farm income stops and incoming supply caravans hold back outside the siege line, so the garrison eats the stockpile down and starts starving when it runs dry.
  • Breaking a siege: starve them out, bring overwhelming force, or break the siege with a relief army, a sally, or by raiding the besiegers' own caravans. Once no units remain inside, the buildings themselves fall in seconds — never leave a town unguarded.
  • Healing: damaged units slowly recover — about 1% of their health per second — while inside your territory, as long as they have food and have been out of combat for a few seconds. Rotate wounded armies home between offensives.
📱 Controls: touch
  • Two modes: two icon buttons sit in the bottom-left: a cursor (Select mode) and a dashed box (Drag mode).
  • Select mode: drag pans, pinch zooms. Tapping a friendly unit opens a small popup asking whether to Select it or Deselect, so a stray tap can't lose your current group; with units already selected the same popup also offers Move here, marching the selection to the tapped unit's spot while keeping it selected.
  • With units selected: tapping open ground moves them right there, tapping a visible enemy blob or settlement attacks it, and tapping one of your own settlements opens a popup with three choices: Select settlement (switch the selection to it), Garrison units (march the group into its walls), or Deselect.
  • Action popup: tap a unit you've already selected to open its action popup — Move, Pillage, Split, Build, Supply route, role switching and Deselect.
  • ✂️ Split: tap for an even split in half, or press and hold it to reveal a slider: slide left or right while still holding, and release to split off exactly that many.
  • Drag mode is only for building a selection: tapping the dashed-box button deselects everything, and from then on each one-finger drag box-selects units, with every new box adding to the group (two fingers still pan and zoom). Tapping one of your settlements in Drag mode switches the selection to that settlement; everything else — moving, attacking, popups — waits until you switch back to Select.
  • Order confirmed: a brief ring at the destination confirms every order (white = move, red = attack).
  • Founding on touch is two-step: tap 🏠 Build in the popup, tap the map to place the outline (re-tap to move it), then tap ✓ to send the founding party.
🖱️ Controls: desktop
  • Mouse: left-click = select/inspect, right-click = move (on an enemy: attack it directly), left-drag = box select, WASD/edge/middle-drag = pan, wheel = zoom, Esc = cancel.
  • Trackpad: two-finger scroll pans, pinch (or Ctrl/Cmd+scroll) zooms.
  • Building: with 🏠 Build armed, a green/red outline follows the mouse and a single click at a valid spot dispatches the founding party.
  • Control groups: Shift+1–9 assigns the selected blobs or settlement to that number; the groups bar on the left edge shows every group as a chip — tap a chip (or press its number) to select it, twice quickly to also center the camera, ✕ on the active chip to clear it, and ＋ to assign the current selection to the next free number (works by touch too).
